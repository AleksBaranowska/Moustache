Moustache

The strip of facial hair grown above the upper lip

Timeline

Brief History of moustache.

2500000 BC

Archaeologists believe cavemen shaved their facial hair into mustaches using shells and tweezers.

2500 BC

Egyptian prince Rahotep from 4th dynasty wears moustache (statue in Egyptian Museum in Cairo).

600 BC

Walrus moustache - an ethnic trait of Celts and Gauls, is characterized by whiskers that are thick, bushy, and drop over the mouth. The style resembles the whiskers of a walrus, hence the name.

300 BC

Image of The Pazyryk Horseman, The Altai Mountains felt artefact (carpet detail) from the Pazyryk burial mounds, a series of Iron Age kurgans in the Altai Mountains, Siberia. Found in the collection of the State Hermitage, St Petersburg.

1535

In England Henry VIII brought back the beard and mustache look.

1585

First time the word moustache recorded in French book "The nauigations, peregrinations and voyages, made into Turkie".

17th century

King Charles I, made the goatee and handlebar moustache iconic, and this was copied by every man of fashion when portraits by Sir Anthony Van Dyck went on display.

1853-1586

Crimean War - facial hair symbol of courage and determination.

1860-1916

Moustache mandatory in British Army.

1910-1960

Moustache retreats, smooth upper lip becomes popular as symbol of emphasis of personal hygiene.

1960-1980

Facial hair as declaration of anticonservatism and old fashioned values, becomes symbol of masculinity.

from 1980

Moustache become symbol of identity - (Army Forces/Police/Fireman).

2010

-->

Moustache comeback as consequence of "movember" movement.

Styles

Chevron

  • Slight upside-down V shape
  • Wider than mouth corners
  • Partially covers upper lip

Lampshade

  • Hair kept off upper lip
  • No wider than mouth
  • Neatly groomed

Walrus

  • Recognizably big and bold
  • Easy to grrom and shape
  • Thick, volumimous

Pencil

  • Just above top lip
  • Same width as mouth
  • Thin, short, devilish

Toothbrush

  • Hair kept off upper lip
  • Shaved sides
  • Don't

Handlebar

  • Thicker moustache style
  • Ends curled upwrds
  • Tips waxed to points

Dali

  • Version of pencil mustache
  • Long ends styled upwards
  • Eccentric

Fu Manchu

  • Straight, thin moustache
  • Wide separation at philtrum
  • Hangs from mouth corners

Horseshoe

  • Shaped like upside-down U
  • Includes cheek growth
  • Often mistaken for a Fu Manchu
